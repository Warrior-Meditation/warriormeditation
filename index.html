Insight Meditation

Insight meditation is meditation in the oldest and most widely practiced form. The practice of insight meditation involves watching the breath, and watching the thoughts and emotions that arise as you attempt to quiet your mind. As you witness these thoughts and emotions you visualize them being released. As you release your thoughts and emotions they cease to have any power over your conscious mind.

Body Scan Meditation

The body scan meditation is based on the practice of Yoga Nidra. The meditator begins by setting an intention for their practice, then watching their breath, and scanning their body. Emotions and thoughts will arise as you do an internal scan of your body, and each time you recognize a discursive thought or emotion and return to the body scan practice, your ability to deal with discursive thoughts and emotions in your everyday life will be greatly improved.

Shapeshifting Meditation

Since the earliest recorded history, meditators have drawn from the natural world as a source for guidance and inspiration. The shamanic practice of shapeshifting allows the meditator to identify and align themselves with the characteristics of an animal or natural element that facilitates separation from your identity of self. It allows you to step outside of yourself as you envision aspects of your mind from the frame of reference of an animal entity. This separation allows for more compassion and more imagination in dealing with your strengths and weaknesses. The goal is the realization that the strengths and weaknesses that you perceive in an animal entity are the same strengths and weaknesses that you perceive in yourself, allowing you to appreciate your strengths and begin to eliminate weaknesses.

What is meditation?

Meditation is a way to develop space between your PERCEPTION OF and your RESPONSE TO external stimulation.

Once you begin to perceive this space you will realize that your mind constantly reacts without your permission!

Something happens, and before you have made a conscious decision, your mind has attached itself to anger, or sadness, or even happiness.

You have been developing these patterns of thought since birth. Meditation gives you a way to reboot your mind and discard old unhelpful patterns.

There is no right way to meditate.

Spiritual and wisdom traditions worldwide agree that you will gain the most benefit by using metaphors that inspire compassion and love for all sentient beings, but this can be done with a broad spectrum of techniques ranging from sitting quietly and watching your breath, following a sound, or imagining spirit animals.

This website will give you an introduction to some basic practices and patterns of thought on which to rest your mind and point yourself in the direction of your own inner space and strength.
